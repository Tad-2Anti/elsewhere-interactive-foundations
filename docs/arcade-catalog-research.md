# Arcade Catalog Research

Verified 18 July 2026. This file is the sourcing ledger for World 01.

## Decision

Arcade launches as an official Nintendo hardware and accessory edit. It does not use emulator boxes, ROM devices, unlicensed reproduction controllers or products whose only distinction is a reseller markup.

## India availability gate

- Nintendo of America's [store-order FAQ](https://en-americas-support.nintendo.com/app/answers/detail/a_id/15578/~/nintendo-store-order-support---faq) says store orders cannot be shipped outside the United States and Canada.
- Nintendo had no official Indian storefront or authorised India launch in the audit. Search results contained grey-market importers and a reported 2027 entry plan; neither is current official availability.
- This is not a permanent claim. Distribution can change, so the store, regional dealer list and Indian launch status must be rechecked before every quote.

## Quote controls

- Confirm the customer's console, account region, membership and supported software.
- Confirm battery-forwarding rules, warranty geography, voltage and Indian wireless/import requirements.
- Never imply that an accessory is a standalone console.
- Ignore unauthorised marketplace availability when making an official-distribution claim, but disclose it if the customer asks about cheaper routes.

## Visual system

Electric violet, cyan, hot pink and acid yellow on near-black. Use grid fragments, translucent controller silhouettes, glossy plastic, cable arcs and tiny UI-like labels. Avoid pixel-font clichés and avoid copying Nintendo characters or package graphics.

## Hero concepts — directions only

1. **After-hours control desk** — Off-centre pile of abstract, unbranded controller forms on torn violet paper; cyan cable loops travel behind the stack, with hot-pink tape and a small red viewing hood. Large quiet black space on the left for the title.
2. **Gravity cabinet** — Eight hardware silhouettes appear to float out of a tilted arcade cabinet aperture. The composition leans hard to the lower-right, with transparent acrylic shadows and one acid-yellow wheel ring breaking the crop.
3. **Playroom signal map** — A bright physical collage of thumbsticks, buttons, a red optical viewer and carrying-case textures connected by hand-drawn route lines. It should feel like a map of ways to play, not a product flat-lay.
