Verified 18 July 2026. This file is the sourcing ledger for World 06.

## Decision

Little focuses on open-ended wooden play for ages three and up. The first edit stays with Giant Bean so materials, warnings and shipping evidence can be evaluated consistently. Baby sleep products, feeding products, magnets, ride-ons and anonymous marketplace toys are excluded.

## India availability gate

- Giant Bean's [official FAQ](https://giantbeankids.com/pages/faq) states that it ships only within the United States and does not offer international shipping.
- No authorised Indian retailer was found for the exact catalog. Marketplace exporters do not count as official Indian stock and must not be used to label an item locally available.
- A negative availability claim expires quickly. Recheck the brand, Indian search results and authorised sellers before every quote.

## Safety and quote controls

- Verify the manufacturer's current age rating, small-parts warning, materials, testing statement and included batteries for the exact SKU.
- Brand-stated ASTM/CPSIA compliance is not a substitute for Indian BIS/customs review where applicable.
- Keep original instructions, packaging and warning labels with the toy.
- Quote bulky freight and the cost of returning damaged flat-pack items before payment.
- Never call a toy educational, Montessori or developmental unless the exact product evidence supports the term.

## Visual system

Sunny yellow, tomato red, cobalt, mint and natural wood on deep violet. Use cut-paper tracks, wooden arches, toy wheels, house sections, crayon marks and confetti with real depth and shadow. Avoid infantile pastels and gendered pink/blue coding.

## Hero concepts — directions only

1. **Tiny city, giant cut paper** — A wooden port, curved track and candy dollhouse collide in an off-centre miniature city on torn violet card. A red crane rises out of frame; yellow confetti and blue roads create movement.
2. **Playroom after the story** — A deliberately messy floor collage: train curve, seven family figures, tiny chair, leaf, wooden spoon and one muddy handprint. Low angled light makes it feel lived-in rather than catalog-perfect.
3. **Build-anything parade** — Toy parts march diagonally upward as a bright procession—wheel, arch, house, crane, animal, spoon—held together by a hand-drawn cobalt track that refuses to stay inside the image frame.
