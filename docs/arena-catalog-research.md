# Arena world — catalog, verification and visual direction

**Research lock:** 18 July 2026  
**World:** Arena · jerseys, performance footwear and useful game-day gear  
**Position:** a sourcing edit beyond the routine Nike/adidas India assortment; not a resale marketplace and not a scarcity feed.

## The availability rule

“Not available in India” is treated as an operational claim, not an aesthetic. A product enters the opening edit only when all of the following checks pass on the research-lock date:

1. the official product or authorised retailer page is live;
2. no official India storefront, India delivery route or authorised Indian stockist is found;
3. no stocked listing is found across the major Indian channels checked: Nike India, adidas India, PUMA India, Decathlon India, Fancode Shop, Myntra, AJIO, VegNonVeg, Superkicks and Basketball Store India;
4. marketplace, forwarding, resale and grey-market listings are ignored as proof of official availability; and
5. the item is rechecked before a customer pays, because sizes, distribution and shipping rules change.

This process cannot prove that no private seller anywhere in India has an item. It verifies the commercially useful claim: **no official Indian retail route was found at the stated date**.

### Confidence note

The SDFC, Sports Fever, Joma and Preach entries have explicit shipping exclusions on first-party or authorised-retailer pages. Moolah Kicks and UNITUS pass the current official-route and authorised-stockist audit but do not publish equally explicit India wording in the pages indexed during research. They must therefore receive a checkout-country recheck before any sourcing quote. If India becomes available, they leave the catalog immediately.

## Items deliberately excluded

- Mainline Nike and adidas shoes or jerseys already represented by their India storefronts.
- IDA Spirit: a good women-specific futsal product, but IDA states that it ships globally through retail partners; it fails the hard India gate.
- Sokito boots: international distribution is broad enough that the India exclusion could not be established confidently.
- StockX, eBay, Ubuy and marketplace imports: useful demand signals, but not authorised availability.
- “Player version” jerseys from unverified Indian stores: authenticity and licensing are unclear.

## Content hierarchy

1. **Jerseys** — provenance first: season, player, licence, retailer and final-sale risk.
2. **Footwear** — surface and fit first: court role, width, brace use, playing surface and size conversion.
3. **Gear** — practical add-ons that improve a consolidated shipment, not filler accessories.

The site must never imply that imported performance footwear prevents injury or performs better for everyone. Every request needs a fit and playing-surface check.

## Hero image concepts — ideas only

### 01 · Locker-room evidence — recommended

A bright, physical scrapbook arranged noticeably off-centre to the lower right: cropped jersey backs, a cobalt basketball outsole, black futsal tread, mesh, laces, a match ticket and handwritten size notes. Acid-lime tape and coral registration marks create energy over a deep bottle-green-to-ink field. The upper-left remains open for the Arena wordmark. Materials should feel photographed and handled—creases, torn paper, stitching and rubber dust—not like floating 3D product renders.

**Palette:** acid lime `#DFFF43`, cobalt `#2855D9`, coral `#E05B3F`, off-white `#F2EEE5`, pitch black `#090B08`.

### 02 · Split court / split pitch

A diagonal collision between varnished hardwood and cropped artificial turf. A basketball shoe enters from the upper-right; a futsal sole and a partial jersey number enter from the lower-left. White court arcs and touchline paint become the collage geometry. Stadium flash, paper grain and one sharp lime sticker keep it youthful; no generic athlete silhouette.

**Best for:** explaining the two-sport scope immediately.  
**Risk:** can become a conventional sports campaign if the physical collage texture is too clean.

### 03 · Collector’s kit wall

A maximal wall of clipped jersey typography, woven labels, numbered provenance cards, socks, wrist tape, a cap panel and two shoe details, all pinned at irregular rotations. One object breaks the frame edge and the full cluster sits right of centre. The background uses faint locker perforations and hand-painted court arrows rather than a plain gradient.

**Best for:** making limited jerseys the lead story.  
**Risk:** requires strict logo and licensing review if reference products are recognisable.

## Image-production guardrails for a later session

- Generate an original editorial collage, not a counterfeit product photograph.
- Do not reproduce club crests, player names, league marks or footwear logos unless licensed source photography is supplied.
- Use abstract jersey panels, numbers, stitching, mesh and outsole shapes to express the category.
- Prepare desktop 4:5, mobile 3:4 and landscape 3:2 crops from one art-directed master.
- Preserve upper-left negative space and a right-weighted object cluster to match the gallery’s off-centre movement.
- No image has been generated or added in this pass; the existing Roam asset remains a temporary placeholder.

## Reverification checklist

- Product page still live and the selected colour/size is stocked.
- Official checkout still rejects India, or the recorded authorised retailer still has no international shipping.
- No authorised Indian distributor has appeared.
- Price, sale status, return rule and final-sale status are refreshed.
- Shoe size, width, brace use and intended surface are recorded.
- Jersey licence, season and customisation status are confirmed.
- Landed-cost quote separates product, domestic shipping, forwarding, duties and service fee.
