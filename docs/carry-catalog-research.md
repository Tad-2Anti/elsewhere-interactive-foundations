# Carry Catalog Research

Verified 18 July 2026. This file is the sourcing ledger for World 03.

## Decision

Carry launches as a Tortuga one-bag system: maximum carry-on backpacks, under-seat and pack-flat day bags, and fitted organisers. The category is not a list of generic travel gadgets, and it excludes hard-shell luggage brands that can manually quote direct India shipping.

## India availability gate

- Tortuga's [official FAQ](https://www.tortugabackpacks.com/pages/faq) tells customers outside the United States to use a freight forwarder. The official store therefore does not ship to India.
- No authorised Indian retailer was found in the audit. Cross-border marketplace or forwarding pages are not treated as domestic stock.
- A negative availability claim can expire. The official checkout, dealer position and Indian search results must be rechecked before every quote.

## Quote controls

- Check airline dimensions against the customer's most-used carriers and fare class, especially when the expandable pack is open.
- Match each pack to torso length, expected packed weight and preference for harness structure; even a two-pack bundle needs two fit checks.
- Confirm laptop dimensions rather than relying on a diagonal screen-size claim.
- Compression reduces volume, not weight; keep the airline mass limit visible.
- Establish a credible defect and warranty-return route before purchase. A worldwide warranty does not make return freight free.

## Visual system

Pool blue, coral, butter yellow, olive and sand on deep navy. Use backpack panels, topographic lines, straps, packing cubes, grid packing and clean blocks of colour. Keep it energetic and modular, not military-tactical or airport-corporate.

## Hero concepts — directions only

1. **Open-pack colour explosion** — A travel backpack sits far to the right, open toward camera. Coral pouches, olive straps, a yellow sock and map fragments burst upward in a controlled arc, leaving a wide pool-blue title field.
2. **One bag, three scales** — Cropped 40-litre pack panels, a flat-fold day bag and packing cubes make a tilted architectural tower. Passport-sized shapes and route stamps act as collage punctuation, with no airline logos or readable destinations.
3. **Packing-grid playground** — An exploded backpack interior becomes a graphic grid of colourful compartments viewed from above, then breaks out of the rectangle with a curved shoulder strap and loose coral zipper pull.
