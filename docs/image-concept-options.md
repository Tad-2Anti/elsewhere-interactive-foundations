# Image concept options

No images are generated or changed in this pass. These directions are for choosing the next art direction before production assets are made.

> Production update, 18 July 2026: the selected direction evolved into a brighter Pop Import Scrapbook system with a plum–cobalt atmospheric paper background. The Adorn portrait, makeup spread, and accessories spread are now implemented; the options below remain the earlier decision record.

## Option 1 — Material evidence (recommended)

**Hero:** A dark, close-cropped still life photographed at table height. Four to five real material fragments from different worlds—handblown amber glass, a cream shadow pot, a brass earring, folded linen and a weathered notebook—sit on a matte black plinth. Hard side-light produces precise shadows; no hands, text, logos or floating objects.

**Homepage worlds:** One tactile, off-centre object study per category, photographed against a rich, nearly black coloured backdrop. Every frame keeps 55–65% negative space so the gallery can move through them without visual noise.

**Why it fits:** It makes the promise feel product-led and credible, while preserving the existing atmospheric depth treatment.

## Option 2 — Collector's worktable

**Hero:** An overhead photograph of a working sourcing desk: annotated cards, material swatches, product tags without visible trademark names, a postage stamp, a folded map and two carefully selected objects. The frame is imperfectly arranged, like a real edit in progress.

**Homepage worlds:** Each world becomes a small working surface: a table setting, a bedside tray, a tea-and-notebook desk, a packed travel pouch, a makeup-and-jewellery tray, and a shelf of curiosities.

**Why it fits:** It explains the service immediately—selection, verification and sourcing—rather than only selling a mood.

## Option 3 — Editorial portrait fragments

**Hero:** A cropped editorial portrait with the person partially obscured: hands, ear, shoulder and objects in frame, using a strong directional flash and a near-black field. The focus is on how products are used, not a generic fashion image.

**Homepage worlds:** Each category uses a different human-scale crop: hands serving, a bedside gesture, a tea pour, a carry strap, an ear/eye makeup crop, and hands turning a book.

**Why it fits:** It is warmer and more aspirational, but requires more careful casting, rights and art direction than the still-life routes.

## Production rules for any option

- Create original imagery or license it clearly; do not use reference-site imagery.
- Avoid visible third-party logos and synthetic-looking impossible objects.
- Use one focal object and substantial negative space per world; the gallery already supplies motion.
- Export portrait-friendly crops first (roughly 4:5), then prepare separate mobile crops where the focal point remains readable.
- Keep colour grading consistent: rich blacks, natural material colour, restrained highlight bloom.
